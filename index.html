Frequently Asked Questions

 Your dogecoin wallet is:
  • You need to fund your wallet to interact on Doge Yip.
  • All sensitive information is handled client-side. We never see it.
  • You can learn more about Dogecoin at Dogecoin.com

The first thing you need to do in Doge Yip is to fund your wallet. This is because all interaction in Doge Yip is done by making transactions in the Dogecoin blockchain. If you do not have any dogecoin in your wallet then you are not able to make any transaction. The reason for this is because Doge Yip uses the dogecoin blockchain as a datastore instead of using a traditional database.

There are several different ways of funding your wallet. The easiest is by simply transfering Dogecoin from an existing wallet to your new Doge Yip Wallet.

A second easy way to fund your new wallet is by going to faucet sites. Faucet sites are typically a page full of ads that send you Dogecoins in exchange for page views. I personally find Free Doge Coin to be the easiest to use but a more comprehensive list of options can be found at the Doge Coin Faucet List.

A third way to fund your new wallet is by buying Dogecoin from an exchange. Here are some trusted exchanges:

  • WeSellDoge(Paypal)
  • ANXPRO (USD, EUR, GBP, AUD, CAD, HKD, SGD, NZD)
  • Kraken (USD, EUR)
  • Celery (USD)
  • LocalDogecoin(A website to purchase Dogecoin locally)

Lastly, the hardest but most anonymous way to fund your new wallet is by mining. Mining is when you use your computer to process Dogecoin transactions by other people, and in return you get Dogecoin. Mining is intended for advanced users only, as it requires a lot of technical knowledge to do. If you are interested, read more here.

Note: These online faucets and exchanges are not owned or maintained by Doge Yip or Dog Yip. While we are satisfied they are trustworthy, reliable services - please use at your own risk.

After funding your wallet the second thing that you should do is to set your username. This allows your account to be searchable so that other people can find you on Doge Yip. It also helps make it easier for people to recognize your user account when you post because all Doge Yip private keys tend to look the same.

You can set your username by going to the set name link in the navagation bar or by clicking here.

Technical:
Behind the scenes setting your username consists of converting a 19 character string to its hexidecimal representation, prepending 0x1E, appending 0x9F, and then base58check encoding the entire string. After that we simply send a transaction to that address. Finally, when Doge Yip loads your recent transactions it simply decodes all the addresses that you have sent dogecoins to and your most recent transaction whose hex value ends in 0x9F becomes your username. Likewise, it becomes trivial to search on this name by simply base58check encoding a searchvalue and looking through all users that have sent dogecoin to that address.

After Setting your name the next thing that you might choose to do is to broadcast your account.

You can broadcast your account by clicking here.

Technical:
Broadcasting your account will make you appear in the Discover page under the Recent Users tab and the Recent News tab. This is essencially a way to bootstrap yourself into the Doge Yip network. This is probably most benificial to do if you are trying to connect with other Doge Yip users while the userbase is still small.

General

What is Doge Yip?

Doge Yip is a decentrialized microbloging service that reads encoded data from the dogecoin blockchain using client-side javascript.

What do I need to start using Doge Yip?

All you need is a dogecoin wallet and some dogecoin. I would suggest going to dogecoin.com to learn more about dogecoin.

How do I post a yip to Doge Yip?

Click on the Yip link in the navagation bar, type whatever you want into the text field, and then send some dogecoin to each of the generated addresses. Once finished the yip is forever associated with the wallet that made the transactions.

Why do I need to pay to post on Dogeyip?

Every post that you make consists of sending dogecoins to multiple dogecoin addresses. This is done because Dogeyip does not have an underlying database. Instead it uses the dogecoin blockchain as a distributated datastore and client-side javascript for decoding this datastore in place of a database. That is what makes this so unique. As long as you have access to the block chain and have some html locally on your computer you can read and write whatever you want and no one can do anything about it.

How do I assign a name to my wallet?

Click on the Set Name link in the navagation bar, type whatever name you want into the text field, and then send some dogecoin to the generated address. Once finished that name is forever associated with the wallet that made the transaction.

Technical

What are the fees to send messages?

To send a message you must make a transaction to the generated address. To do so you would send over 1 Doge to the generated address and another Doge to a miner as incentive to put your transaction into the block chain. Note that the generated address has no known private key. Thus sending a Doge to that address is equivalent to burning a Doge.

What's the maximum message length?

There isn't a theoretical limit. Currently you are limited by how many Dogecoins you have.

Won't this bloat the blockchain?

Potentially. But if a transaction amount is too small then no miner should actually write it into the block chain. I would think that it would produce no more bloat than the faucet sites.
